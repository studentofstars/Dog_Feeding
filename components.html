Hardware Components

These are the essential components used to build our automated dog feeder

ESP32 CAM WiFi Module with OV2640 Camera

The brain of our system, this WiFi-enabled microcontroller with integrated camera allows remote monitoring and control of the feeder.

SG90 Micro Servo Motor

Used to control the food dispensing mechanism, this small but powerful motor enables precise control over food portions.

Ultra Sonic Sensor

Detects the presence of dogs near the feeder, triggering the camera and enabling smart dispensing based on proximity.

IR Sensor

Works in conjunction with the ultrasonic sensor for more accurate detection, especially in low light conditions.

3-6V Mini Submersible Water Pump

Provides fresh water to accompany the food, ensuring dogs receive hydration along with nutrition.

Two Channel Relay

Controls higher voltage components like the water pump, allowing the microcontroller to safely manage these devices.

FTDI 232 Mini USB to TTL Converter

Used for programming the ESP32 CAM module and debugging the system during development.

Breadboard

Provides a platform for prototyping and connecting the various electronic components without soldering.

Power Bank

Powers the entire system, allowing for portable deployment and operation without direct connection to electricity.

Female to Female Jumper Wires

Connect components with female headers to each other, creating electrical pathways in the circuit.

Male to Male Jumper Wires

Connect components with male headers or breadboard connections together in the circuit.

Male to Female Jumper Wires

Connect components with male headers to those with female headers, providing versatile connectivity options.

100uF/25V Electrolytic Capacitor

Stabilizes the power supply and prevents voltage spikes that could damage sensitive components.

330 Ohm Resistor

Limits current flow to LEDs and other components, ensuring they operate within safe parameters.

USB A Type to Micro USB B

Connects the power bank to the system, providing the necessary power for operation.
