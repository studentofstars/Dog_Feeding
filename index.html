Project Abstract

The integration of Internet of Things (IoT) with digital platforms offers new possibilities for improving animal welfare and promoting community-driven support. This project presents a Smart Dog Feeding and Monitoring System combined with a web-based application for donations and adoption. The hardware unit, built using an ESP32 microcontroller and ESP32-CAM module, automates feeding and water dispensing while simultaneously capturing real-time images of street dogs.

These images are uploaded to the web application, where users can monitor dogs, contribute through donations, and even initiate adoption processes. The donation interface ensures transparency and encourages greater participation by allowing individuals to directly support animal care initiatives.

By uniting automated pet care with digital engagement, the system not only ensures timely nutrition and monitoring for street dogs but also fosters a collaborative platform that connects communities, donors, and potential adopters. The project highlights the role of IoT and web technologies in addressing social challenges, bridging the gap between technology, compassion, and sustainable animal welfare.
